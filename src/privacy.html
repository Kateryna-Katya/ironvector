Privacy Policy

Introduction

This notice sets out the basis on which any personal data we collect from you, or that you provide to us, will be processed by us. Please read the following carefully to understand our views and practices regarding your sensitive information and how we will deal with it. For the purposes of the Data Protection Act (‘the DPA’) and the EU General Data Protection Regulation (‘the GDPR’), sensitive information includes what is defined as your ‘personal data’.

By visiting ironvector.online (the ‘Website’) you accept and consent to the practices described in this Privacy Notice including the processing of your personal data under this lawful basis.
In this Privacy Notice, we seek to abide by the letter and spirit of the guidelines laid out by the UK Information Commissioner’s Office in the document ‘Privacy Notices, Transparency and Control’.

Who We Are

We are registered with the Information Commissioners Office under registration number ZA388035.

For the purposes of the DPA and in-line with the GDPR, Iron Vector: Impact Grid is the Data Controller.

If you have any concerns about the way we use your information or any questions about this Privacy Notice, please let us know. We can be contacted via email at info@ironvector.online.

What we do with your information

We will use your information to:

  • Send you updates about our games and other information we feel may be of interest to you;
  • Improve our Website, our games and our overall user experience; and
  • Deliver information about the services we provide by other means.

We compile statistics about user trends on our Website, which are used by third-party organizations to understand how users interact with businesses, brands and one another online and to advise about these things. These statistics are drawn from a dataset which does not contain any information from which you can be identified.

If you are an existing user of our Website prior to the date of this Notice then any communication will be delivered to you on the legal basis of legitimate interests.

Information we collect from you

We will collect and process the following information about you:

Information you give us – this is information about you that you give us by filling in forms on our Website or by corresponding with us by phone, email or otherwise. We only request from you the minimum data required for you to use our service.

Email Subscription – The only information we require from you to sign up to our mailing list is your email address.
Contact Form – In order for us to respond to you efficiently we store your name, email address and message. We retain this data in our website database for audit trail and historical evidence purposes.

Job Application Form – In order for us to respond to your job application, we store your name, email address, date of birth, phone number, portfolio link and relevant information in our website database for audit trail and to contact you about future roles which may be of interest.

Game play – through your use of our games, we will receive information about you from the platform through which you are accessing that game. This information is limited to the console/platform that you’re playing on and the country in which you’re located. This information will not identify you individually.

Specifically for our Meta Quest titles, we require access to the following data from players for our games:

  • User ID (Grants an app access to user id to enable various features)
  • User Profile (Grants an app access to the Oculus username and profile photo)
  • Friends List for online titles (Grants an app access to a user’s friends list. This is needed to enable certain platform features)
  • Deep Linking (Grants an app access to view a user’s desired destination within an app)

Studio/User Interaction – from time to time, you may submit crash/bug reports to us, which will include a report generated by the game you’re playing that documents what might have gone wrong together with some information about your setup (hardware, operating system, version numbers, etc). In doing so, you may provide screenshots showing the issue. The tool you use for this purpose should recognise and obscure any information on show that may identify you before transmitting the screenshot to us but we cannot guarantee that it will. However, if it comes to our attention that such screenshots include such information, we will immediately take steps to remove that information from our systems.

Whenever you play our games, depending on the game and the platform, we will collect:

  • Details concerning the device or console and platform on which it operates; and/or the country in which you are located.

We will obtain this information from the platform that you’re using to play the game.It will not identify you as an individual.

Whenever you visit our Website we will automatically collect the following information:

Information about your visit – this includes the full Uniform Resource Locators (URL), clickstream to, through and from our Website (including date and time), pages you have visited, page response times, download errors, length of visits to certain page and page interaction information (such as scrolling, clicks, and mouse-overs).

Information we receive from other sources – This is information we receive about you if you use any of the other websites we operate or the other services we provide. In this case we will have informed you when we collected that data if we intend to share those data internally and combine it with data collected on this Website. We will also have told you for what purpose we will share and combine your data. We are working closely with third parties (including, for example, business partners, sub-contractors in technical, payment and delivery services, advertising networks, analytics providers, search information providers, credit reference agencies). We will notify you when we receive information about you from them and the purposes for which we intend to use that information.

Where we hold and what we do with your information

Information that we hold that is derived from or concerns your game play will usually come to us via the platform on which you are playing the game in question.

Information we receive will be stored in the UK or on other servers located in the European Union and dispersed geographically to ensure that it remains as resilient as reasonably possible.

The information that we received is aggregated with that of other players of the game on the platform you’re using and in the country in which you are located. It will be used for us to assess the sales we’re making in order to inform our marketing and commercial strategies.

The information and content held on our Website is deployed geographically to maximize user experience. We will never share your user information with third parties for promotional purposes.

Our website is hosted by OVH who’s policies you can view here. Any data you submit on our Website will be stored within the MySQL database for the website.

Our newsletters and email marketing are powered by MailChimp. If you want to learn more about MailChimp and how they handle your personal data then please view their website here.

We may also disclose your information to third parties to whom we may choose to sell, transfer, or merge parts of our business or our assets.
Alternatively, we may seek to acquire other businesses or merge with them. If a change happens to our business, then the new owners may use your personal data in the same way as set out in this Privacy Notice.
